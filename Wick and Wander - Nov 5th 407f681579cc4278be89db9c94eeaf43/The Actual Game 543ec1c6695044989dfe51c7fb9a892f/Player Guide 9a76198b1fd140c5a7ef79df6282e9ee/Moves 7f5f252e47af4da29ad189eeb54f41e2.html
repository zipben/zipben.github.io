Moves

Common Moves

  1. Think back: When you want to remember something you haven’t thought about for a while, test with Lore.
  1. Pick out the details: When you want to investigate an area in search of particular information, test with Lore
  1. Listen to the Weave: When you want to sense other living creatures nearby, test with Harmony
  1. Find your limits: When you push your body or mind to its limit, test with Power
  1. See yourself in another: When you want to understand someones intentions, or convince them of something, test with Empathy
  1. Quicken: When you must make a decision or take an action with no time to waste, test with Grace
  1. Don’t make a sound: When you want to avoid being detected, test with Grace
  1. Dodge: When you are the target of violence and want to avoid injury while putting distance between you and your attacker, test with Grace
  1. Engage: When someone or something threatens you, and you wish to fight back, test with Power or Grace.
  1. Kill: When you use violence against a living creature with the goal of ending it’s life, test for a seed. Explain how the act affects your relationship with the seed.

Special Moves

  1. Gather round the fire: When you make camp, you sit together and discuss the events of the day. You may add 1 seed to your character deck for someone who helped you that day.
  1. Renew: When you make camp, you may use a botanical catalyst to open your mind and heal your body. Take a seed from your deck and add it to your pile of resolved seeds. Take an injury from your character deck and return it to your injury deck. Briefly explain how the seed helped you overcome the trauma of the injury.
  1. Return to the weave: When your injury deck is exhausted you fall unconscious, check if it is your time to return to the weave. Shuffle your character deck. Draw until you encounter a seed or an injury.
    1. If you draw a seed, explain how that person pulled you back from the brink, remove 1 injury from your deck, you are now conscious.
    1. If you draw an injury card, your character has died, describe how they pass, and which form they take in death.
  1. Take a Seed: When you encounter a bodum, you may harvest some piece of it, and after consuming it, add a seed to your character deck. The Lore Keeper should help explain the memory you’ve found.

Community Moves

  1. Rest: When you arrive at home, remember that home means comfort and safety, indulge in that comfort in whatever way best suits your character. Remove all injury from your deck, and shuffle.
  1. Prepare for the journey ahead: When you are getting together what you need for a long journey, test with Lore or Empathy
  1. Speak with the Elders: When you wish to know something about the area around a community, test with Lore
  1. Meditate: At the end of a session, when your player has an opportunity to sit and reflect, you must exchange resolved seeds for attribute cards.
  1. Come in from the cold: At the end of a session, if a courier has 12 or more of a single attribute, they become an elder. Create a seed card for your elder, and add it to the Lore Keeper’s Deck. Add one attribute card to the Lore Keepers deck of the players choice.
