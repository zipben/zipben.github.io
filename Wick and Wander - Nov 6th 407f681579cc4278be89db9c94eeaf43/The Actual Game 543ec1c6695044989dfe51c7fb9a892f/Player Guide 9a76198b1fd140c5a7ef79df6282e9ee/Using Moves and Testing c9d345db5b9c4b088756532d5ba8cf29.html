Using Moves and Testing

Wick and Wander is a card driven TTRPG that uses a custom deck of attribute cards to capture a characters nature. It is inspired by PbtA and so uses moves to capture what characters might want to do while exploring the world. Each character has access to a set of common moves, special moves, community moves and a small set of moves associated with their personal Archetype.

As players wander through the world, they may want to attempt particular actions, or be forced to by circumstance. Players and the Lore Keeper (LK) work together to find a move that captures the players intent. The player then uses that move, which will typically involve a test.

Testing

This is the primary mechanic in WnW, equivalent to rolling dice in most other games.

A test requires a target attribute (With the notable exception of the Kill move which targets a seed) which is typically established by the move.

  • Declare the attribute you are testing for
  • Draw cards from your shuffled character deck, moving them to your discard pile as you draw them. Continue until one of the following things happen. Tests cannot be stopped in any other way than those listed below.
    • You draw the attribute you are looking for - Count the number of cards flipped and follow the instructions on the complications table
    • You draw a Seed - Seeds are considered wild during a test. This is equivalent to finding the attribute you are testing for, but must include a narrative rationale for how the seed (typically a person) factors into the resulting narrative.
    • You draw an injury card - The test ends with a major complication
    • You exhaust your deck - The test ends with a major complication. After you have resolved any complications, shuffle your discard pile back into your draw deck.

Degrees of Success

(1) Complete Success: You succeed beyond your wildest dreams. You get what you were after and then some. Describe how you achieve your goal, and what additional boon accompanies your success

(2-3) Partial Success with a complication: You get some or all of what you were after, but things get complicated.

  • The player chooses a complication

(4+) Major Complication: You may get some of what you were after, but things likely get much worse

  • The GM picks two complications

Complications

Choose a complication that corresponds to the attribute you are testing for.

  • Universal
    • You injure yourself
    • You imperil someone else
    • You need help - Another player takes a move.
  • Lore
    • What you learned scares you
    • You are left with more questions than answers
    • You misunderstand something in a dangerous way
  • Empathy
    • Someone is annoyed with you until you find a way to apologize
    • Someone learns something about you that you did not want to share
    • Someone becomes violent
  • Harmony
    • The weave lashes out
    • Something hears you
    • You destabilize the area
  • Power
    • You break something
    • You overshoot your mark
    • You leave yourself vulnerable
  • Grace
    • You stumble
    • You draw unwanted attention to yourself
    • You get turned around
  • Seed
    • Your relationship with the person grows tense
