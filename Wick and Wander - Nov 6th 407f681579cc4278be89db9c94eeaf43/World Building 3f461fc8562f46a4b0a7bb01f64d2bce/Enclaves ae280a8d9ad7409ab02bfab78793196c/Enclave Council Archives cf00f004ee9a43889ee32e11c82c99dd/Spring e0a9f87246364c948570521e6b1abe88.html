Spring

Entry 1

  • The Gardening Guild has agreed to help the agricultural cohorts that tend the outer most fields of the borderlands in order to till and seed essential food crops more efficiently.
  • The Spring market has been moved to the fall storage pavilion for the next 3 months as the community market hall has its roof repaired after the unseasonal snowfall causes the far eastern edge to collapse.
  • Keil Sunrest, the Steward of the Teaching Guild, unfortunately passed before any of the younger teachers completed their guild training. Stewards from other guilds are working together to train Sole Freeda into the role. Keil’s bodum is a lemon tree and has been planted in the grove outside the guild hall. Hopefully Sole likes lemons.
  • The community store of Lua Seeds is lower than expected after the unexpectedly cold winter. Couriers have been selected to travel east to Graveholme in order to replenish our stock for next winter.

Entry 2

  • Hunting along the eastern borderlands is proving difficult since the blind was taken over by a family of Krek this past winter and they are now nesting. The Council was split on clearing them out or building a new blind since they now have hatchlings, but given the ample game along the northern border, it was decided that the Krek could have the blind and a crew will be sent to build a new one in a few weeks time.
  • A large hornets nest was found in the space between two homes next to the library. Normally we would wait until the Wilding Guild had someone available after the seed harvest, but the cohort in one of the houses is primarily elder community members and there is a concern over their safety. The Wilding steward said they’d have a small crew return in a days time.
  • A group of young, unguilded community members once again petitioned for the formation of an antiquities guild with a focus on studying pre awakening artifacts, and were once again denied despite calling for a community wide vote instead of a proxy vote. The high bar for “does this serve the community” is proving difficult for them to clear as none of the artifacts they have demonstrated do any particular job better than our standard approaches.

Entry 3

  • The Wellness guild is asking for help recruiting unguilded community members in anticipation of a small surge in child births 7 months from now.
    • “The winter was particularly productive for the community” -Belle Faite, Wellness Steward
  • A Peregrine named Lak Fife, who joined us for the winter, has announced their departure at the end of this month after the morning frost breaks. The community is preparing a small farewell breakfast.

Entry 4

  • Brem Statcher passed on during the winter, leaving the northern most homestead empty. He’d not been heard from since the mid winter but he liked his privacy so that was not surprising. A hunting party found his home empty. Later they found a bodum on the ridge at the far edge of the farm where the borderlands meet the wilds. It was a crab apple tree, which seems appropriate. They were able to confirm it was Brem. A service will be conducted next week.
  • The council has called for a community vote on what to do with the homestead Brem was tending. It’s been largely non-productive for years since he was alone out there, and none of the larger cohorts want to be that close to the edge of the stonefield. The council’s proposal is to allow it to rewild and become an stonecovered hunting ground for new members of the guild to learn in.

Entry 5

  • Our supply of pinewort salve is running low after 14 children and 4 of our youngest Seedling guild members were stricken with Pickenpocks. The Seedling guild has committed to inoculating all new members against Pickenpocks going forward given their prximity to the youngest members of our community.
  • Sunshine Boldwater, son to our Council head Sarah Boldwater, has petitioned to open a bakery in the old, and long unused print house on the southern edge of the fountain square. The space is in need of repair so part of the petition is for community support in patching walls and ceiling as well as building a new oven in the back. Councilor Boldwater recused herself from the vote. Despite that, it passed unanimously, aided, I am sure, but the thumbprint cookies provided by the younger Boldwater.
  • A surly Peregrine named Jackson Hornweather arrived 3 days ago and took lodging in the community center. After asking for bread, cheese, water, and a pencil, he was not seen until this morning when he dropped a stack of journal pages with the Archivist and left town.
  • The Calcifer river flooded as it always does this time of year, bringing with it deadfall trees. One of those trees, larger than is typical, caught on the riverwalk and cracked on of the central support posts. The construction guild needs to assess the extent of the damage before the walk can be reopened.
